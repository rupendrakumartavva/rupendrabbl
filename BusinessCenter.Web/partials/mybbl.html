My Basic Business Licenses

Help With This Page (Click Here)

This page is where all online Basic Business Licenses are applied for, renewed, displayed and managed.

  • Return to this page to access your BBL applications and to renew existing licenses online.
  • This page will be blank until an application is in progress or an existing license is added to the page and is ready for renewal.

To Apply for a New License select the button. Instructions on the next steps will be provided on each page.

To Renew an Existing License online, you must have a PIN number from the renewal letter sent from DCRA. Renewal letters are sent 90 days prior to the license expiration date.

Once you have received a renewal letter, select the button. Instructions on the next steps will be provided on each page.

To Continue an Application in Progress select the button to resume work on the application.

To View and Print Licenses and Permits select the icon to open a printable version of approved Basic Business Licenses and Expedited Home Occupancy Permits (EHOP).

To Filter the View of Licenses select a blue button to show only those licenses in each category or type a filter word in the keyword search field. Select "Clear Filter" to clear the filter view.

Glossary of Terms for Licenses Displayed on this Page

  • License/Application #: This field will display the unique identifier associated with the user's un-submitted application in progress, submitted application under review, and approved or existing license number
    • DAPP: the alpha-numeric identifier for an un-submitted draft application.
    • LAPP or IAPP: the alpha-numeric identifier for an application that has been successfully submitted and is under review.
    • License Number: the numeric identifier of an existing or approved license.
  • Expiration Date: This field will display the expiration date of a license that has been approved.
  • Licensee (First & Last Name): The field will display name of the applicant.
  • Business Name: This field will display the name of the your Business.
  • Submitted Date: This field will display the date that you successfully paid and submitted the application for review or renewal.
  • Status Date: This field will display the date of the most recent change in the status in your license application or business license (see Status for a list of status types, below)
  • Trade Name: This field will display the trade name of the business, if applicable.
  • Premise Address: This field will display the Address of the business that is being licensed.
  • License Categories: This field will display the license category of the application.
  • Status: This field will display the status of your application. The types of statuses are:
    • Draft Application in Progress: A partially completed application that has not been submitted to DCRA for review.
    • Under Review: A completed application that has been submitted to DCRA with supporting documents requiring review and validation.
    • Deficient: A completed application that has been reviewed and has been returned to the customer with a request for corrections or additional information.
    • Abandoned: An application with one or more deficiencies that were not resolved within 30 calendar days of the original submission date. Fees are not refundable for applications that were Abandoned. The customer will have to submit a new application if they wish to receive a business license.
    • Denied: An application that did not comply with all requirements for the specific license category, including but not limited to Clean Hands requirements, failed inspections, failed background checks, etc.
    • Appealed: An application originally denied that has been appealed by the customer.
    • Active: A business license with more than 90 days prior to its expiration date.
    • Expiring Soon: A license within 90 days or less of its expiration date that is eligible for renewal.
    • Lapsed: A license between one to thirty days past its expiration date will be assessed a $250.00 penalty fee in addition to the renewal fee.
    • Expired: A license between 31 to 180 days past its expiration date will be assessed a $500.00 penalty fee in addition to the renewal fee.
    • Unable to Renew: A license that expired more than 180 days ago may not be renewed. The business owner must apply for a new license and pay all outstanding fees on the expired license.
    • Canceled: A license that has been canceled by the customer—typically when a business has ceased operations.

If you need additional help, review the My BBLs User Guide or contact the DCRA Help Desk at (202) 442-4400.
